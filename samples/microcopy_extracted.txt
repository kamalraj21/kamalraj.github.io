Sample 1: Interface-Microcopy-Sample 
Title: Interface Microcopy Examples 
Introduction: 
Microcopy plays a crucial role in guiding users through an application. The following examples 
highlight best practices in writing concise, user-friendly, and context-specific microcopy for 
various interface elements. 
Onboarding Screens: 
• 
Welcome Message: 
o Original: "Welcome to our platform! Let's get started by setting up your 
profile." 
o Revised: "Welcome! Ready to set up your profile?" 
o Explanation: Simplified the message, reduced word count, and added a 
friendly tone. 
• 
Action Button: 
o Original: "Click here to continue" 
o Revised: "Continue" 
o Explanation: Clear and action-oriented language for more effective guidance. 
Error Messages: 
• 
Form Submission Error: 
o Original: "The input you provided is incorrect. Please try again." 
o Revised: "Oops! Something’s not right. Please check and try again." 
o Explanation: Using a more conversational tone helps to reduce user 
frustration and encourages them to reattempt. 
• 
Password Requirements: 
o Original: "Password does not meet the requirements." 
o Revised: "Password must be at least 8 characters with one number." 
o Explanation: Provides specific guidance to help users understand the 
requirements. 
Tooltips: 
• 
Password Field: 
o Original: "Enter your password." 
o Revised: "Choose a strong password—at least 8 characters." 
o Explanation: Offers actionable advice to support a positive user experience. 
Confirmation Messages: 
• 
Account Creation: 
o Original: "Your account has been successfully created." 
o Revised: "Success! Your account is ready." 
o Explanation: Adds a positive tone and confirms success succinctly. 
Conclusion: 
Effective microcopy improves user interaction by providing clarity, guidance, and a touch of 
personality to the interface.
