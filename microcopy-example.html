Interface Microcopy Examples

Showcasing the impact of clear, concise, and human-friendly UI text.

Introduction

Microcopy plays a crucial role in guiding users through an application. The following examples highlight best practices in writing concise, user-friendly, and context-specific microcopy for various interface elements.

Onboarding Screens

Welcome Message

Original

"Welcome to our platform! Let's get started by setting up your profile."

Revised

"Welcome! Ready to set up your profile?"

Why it works: Simplified the message, reduced word count, and added a friendly tone.

Action Button

Original

"Click here to continue"

Revised

"Continue"

Why it works: Clear and action-oriented language for more effective guidance.

Error Messages

Form Submission Error

Original

"The input you provided is incorrect. Please try again."

Revised

"Oops! Something’s not right. Please check and try again."

Why it works: Using a more conversational tone helps to reduce user frustration and encourages them to reattempt.

Password Requirements

Original

"Password does not meet the requirements."

Revised

"Password must be at least 8 characters with one number."

Why it works: Provides specific guidance to help users understand the requirements directly at the point of failure.

Tooltips

Password Field

Original

"Enter your password."

Revised

"Choose a strong password—at least 8 characters."

Why it works: Offers actionable advice to support a positive user experience rather than stating the obvious.

Confirmation Messages

Account Creation

Original

"Your account has been successfully created."

Revised

"Success! Your account is ready."

Why it works: Adds a positive tone and confirms success succinctly.

Conclusion

Effective microcopy improves user interaction by providing clarity, guidance, and a touch of personality to the interface.
